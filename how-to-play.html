Filler is a two-player color-control game played on a fixed 8 by 7 board. You start in the bottom-left corner, your opponent starts in the top-right corner, and each turn is a choice between the colors neither player currently owns.

The Starting Position

Your first square is the bottom-left corner and starts as black. Your opponent's first square is the top-right corner and starts as purple. You always move first.

The board is always 8 columns wide and 7 rows tall, using the same six colors: red, green, yellow, blue, purple, and black.

How a Turn Works

  1. Choose any color except your current color and your opponent's current color.
  2. All squares you already own change to the color you picked.
  3. You capture any touching square of that color connected to your territory.
  4. Only horizontal and vertical connections count. Diagonal squares do not connect.
  5. You cannot capture squares that your opponent already owns.

Winning the Game

Players alternate turns until every square belongs to someone. The winner is the player with the most squares at the end. If both players finish with the same number of squares, the game is a draw.

Basic Strategy

Grow through clusters

Large connected groups are usually more valuable than a single isolated square.

Watch the border

Moves that create more future options can matter more than the biggest immediate gain.

Block key colors

Your color choice also removes that color from your opponent's next legal choices.

Count the endgame

Late turns are often about denying a capture path instead of expanding quickly.
